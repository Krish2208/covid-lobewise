About Us

We developed a deep-learning-based method to overcome the limitations of manual chest CT scoring systems and sheds new light on the correlation of biochemical parameters with lung inflammation in COVID-19 patients. The COVID data was collected from Kalinga Institute of Medical Sciences by the enthusiastic team from KIMS and IIT Indore. The study is published in the prestigious IEEE Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics and comprises more than 1100 COVID-19 patients. It found that C-reactive protein (CRP) is the most critical parameter for classifying symptomatic and asymptomatic groups. To overcome the limitations of the manual chest CT scoring system, the researchers used a 2D U-Net-based deep learning (DL) approach to segment the lungs and detect ground-glass-opacity (GGO) in specific lobes from 2D CT images. This method showed over 90% accuracy, compared to the manual process (around 80%), which is subjected to the radiologist's experience. The findings suggest that the effect of inflammation-mediated disease progression may start initially from the right lobe region of the lungs and subsequently affect the rest of the lobes. The group concludes that their approach can be helpful in the early detection of lung involvement in COVID-19 prior to performing unnecessary CT scans. Further study on geographically diverse large population may help to understand the association of the biochemical parameters and pattern of GGO in lung lobes with different SARS-CoV-2 Variants of Concern's disease pathogenesis in these populations.
